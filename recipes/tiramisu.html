Tiramisu

Description

To make tiramisu, start by whisking together 3 large egg yolks and ½ cup of sugar until pale and creamy. Gently fold in 250 g of mascarpone cheese until smooth. In a separate bowl, whip 1 cup of heavy cream until soft peaks form, then fold it into the mascarpone mixture. Prepare 1 cup of strong coffee and let it cool, optionally adding 1–2 tablespoons of coffee liqueur. Quickly dip ladyfinger biscuits into the coffee — don’t soak them or they’ll fall apart.

Arrange a layer of dipped ladyfingers in the bottom of a dish, then spread half of the mascarpone mixture on top. Repeat with another layer of dipped ladyfingers and the remaining mascarpone cream. Smooth the surface, cover, and refrigerate for at least 4–6 hours (overnight is best) to let the flavors meld. Just before serving, dust generously with cocoa powder.

Ingredients

  • 3 large egg yolks
  • ½ cup sugar
  • 250 g mascarpone cheese
  • 1 cup heavy cream
  • 1 cup strong brewed coffee (cooled)
  • 1–2 tablespoons coffee liqueur (optional)
  • 200 g ladyfinger biscuits
  • Unsweetened cocoa powder (for dusting)

Steps

  1. Whisk egg yolks and sugar until pale and creamy.
  2. Fold in mascarpone cheese until smooth.
  3. In another bowl, whip the heavy cream until soft peaks form.
  4. Gently fold whipped cream into the mascarpone mixture.
  5. Brew strong coffee, let it cool, and stir in liqueur if using.
  6. Briefly dip each ladyfinger into the coffee.
  7. Arrange half the dipped biscuits in a serving dish.
  8. Spread half the mascarpone mixture over them.
  9. Repeat with another layer of biscuits and cream.
  10. Cover and refrigerate for 4–6 hours, ideally overnight.
  11. Dust with cocoa powder before serving.
